Resume Tips

  • Try to keep your resume on one page if possible.
  • Make sure to highlight things in order of importance with clear titles and headings.
  • Use a templates and fonts that are easy to read.
  • Only put things you’re ready to talk about, as well as put things that will distinguish you than all of the others.
  • When talking about projects, “I accomplished X, relative to Y, by doing Z using technology T, language L.”
  • Put your github link as well as project descriptions.
  • List languages in order from what you know best to last.

Technical Interview Tips - Process

  • Pick the language that you are most comfortable with.
  • Don’t say silent for too long while implementing your solution.
  • Walk the person you’re interviewing with through your thought process first and get feedback before you start writing the code on the board.
  • Start discussing the problem and your solution - “First thing that comes to mind…”
  • Optimize your solution after you complete it.
  • Even if you don’t get a question correct, explain in depth how and why you chose your solution, this will help them understand that you have the knowledge.

Technical Interview Tips - Keep in mind

  • What data structures can help solve this problem?
  • What common algorithms can I employ?
  • What is the runtime of my solution? How can I decrease the complexity?
  • If I use X sort or search, what is its runtime? Why is it so?
  • What are some corner cases that I have to consider?
